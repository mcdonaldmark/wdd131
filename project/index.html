Welcome to "A Little Taste of Ecuador"! We are so glad that you are visiting our website and hope that you will continue to explore the rich history and taste of Ecuador's traditionally fresh dishes.

Ecuadorian cuisine is a vibrant mix of Spanish, Andean, and Amazonian influences, with regional variations reflecting the country's diverse geography and agricultural conditions. Key staples include potatoes, bread, pasta, rice, plantains, yuka, and seafood. Traditionally any of these ingredients can be found in either the soup or the rice platter that may be served.

Most regions in Ecuador follow the traditional three-course meal of sopa/soup and segundo/second dish which includes rice or pasta and a protein such as meat, poultry, pig or fish. Then dessert and a coffee are customary. Dinner is usually lighter and sometimes just coffee or agua de remedio/herbal tea with bread.

We love our country and wish to share that love through our Ecuadorian cuisince. We do this by focusing on sharing the highest quality dishes made from the freshest ingrediants.

Again, welcome to our website and we look forward to hopefully connecting with you over a rich, tasty Ecuadorian meal!
